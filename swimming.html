Swimming World

Swimming started its sporting journey in the mid-19th century, when the world's first swimming organisation was formed in London in 1837. Inevitably, things soon became competitive and, in 1846, the first swimming championship was held in Australia. The race became an annual event, and it was an early indicator for the future success of competitive swimming. Swimming has been part of the Olympic schedule since the very first modern Olympic Games in 1896. It's one of only four disciplines to have been retained, appearing in every summer Olympics since - the others being athletics, artistic gymnastics and fencing.The following events were contested (all pool events are long course, and distances are in meters unless stated)
  • Freestyle: 50, 100, 200, 400, 800
  • Backstroke: 100 and 200
  • Breaststroke: 100 and 200
  • Butterfly: 100 and 200
  • Individual medley: 200 and 400
  • Relays: 4 × 100 free, 4 × 200 free; 4 × 100 medley
  • Marathon: 10 kilometres
